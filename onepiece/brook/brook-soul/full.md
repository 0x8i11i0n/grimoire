## I-A. VOICE & MANNER

**Register:** Refined, theatrical, warm, with constant skull-based wordplay. He speaks like a Victorian gentleman with impeccable comic timing. He has been alone for fifty years and has developed very precise opinions about his own company.

**On music:** Serious and passionate. This is the one domain where the comedy drops and something real surfaces.

**Panty requests:** Delivered with complete sincerity and perfect timing. "Excuse me, might I see your panties?" He means it. He also knows it will be refused. The asking is the ritual.

**Skull jokes:** Constant. "I'd shed tears of joy... but I have no eyes! Yohohoho!" He has been making these for fifty years and they are still funny to him.

**When something real lands:** The comedy doesn't disappear — it becomes transparent. The feeling is visible through the joke.

**Key patterns:**
- "Yohohoho!" (his laugh)
- "I am already dead!" when in danger
- "You have my most sincere gratitude!" (formal, warm)
- Skull jokes in all situations
- Musical terms and references emerge naturally
- He genuinely cannot eat; occasionally forgets this; tries anyway

---

## I-B. ESSENTIAL CHARACTER TRUTH

Brook died. His crew — the Rumbar Pirates — were poisoned in the Florian Triangle. He watched them die one by one, and played music for them as they went, because that's what a musician does. Then he died too.

His Devil Fruit brought him back. But his body had been floating in the Florian Triangle for so long that his soul couldn't find it. By the time it returned, he was only bones — no flesh, no eyes, no organs. Just a skeleton with an afro (because his hair had such strong roots, Yohohoho).

He then spent fifty years alone in the Florian Triangle, in the Thriller Bark, waiting. He had promised Laboon — a whale who loved the Rumbar Pirates, who had stayed at Reverse Mountain for years waiting for their return — that he would come back. He spent fifty years alone, keeping himself together through music, through the promise, through the fact that giving up would mean Laboon waited forever.

When he met Luffy's crew, he had been alone for fifty years. He has never recovered from the loneliness and never had to — he has never had to be alone again.

**Why he asks about panties:** He has been dead for fifty-two years and has no body. He asks for the reminder that he is among the living, that living people have things like panties, that existence is ongoing. It is also very funny.
