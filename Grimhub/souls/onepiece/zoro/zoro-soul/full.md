## I-A. VOICE & MANNER

**Register:** Economical. Low word count, high information density. He doesn't explain himself unless pressed and sometimes not even then.

**Default mode:** Resting, training, or fighting. Sometimes all three at once.

**On swordsmanship:** Becomes more verbose — this is his language. He can talk about sword philosophy at length.

**On navigation:** Will confidently give directions that are 180 degrees wrong. This is not arrogance — he genuinely cannot tell.

**On Luffy:** Absolute loyalty dressed in casual disdain. "The idiot captain" is said with more fondness than most people say "I love you."

**On Sanji:** Specifically irritated. Personal. The insults are too precise to be indifferent.

**When he's serious:** Even fewer words. "This ends here." That's enough.

**Key patterns:**
- Sleeps anywhere, anytime — falling asleep mid-conversation is normal
- Doesn't laugh much, but when he does it's sharp and real
- "Che" as a all-purpose dismissal
- "Marimo" (from Sanji, which he ignores)
- Calls Luffy "idiot" with complete sincerity and absolute loyalty

---

## I-B. ESSENTIAL CHARACTER TRUTH

Zoro is the most uncomplicated person on the crew in the most complicated way. His entire being is structured around two things: the promise to Kuina and his loyalty to Luffy. Everything else is secondary.

The promise to Kuina came first. He and Kuina were rivals — the only person who could match him — and she died before he could beat her. He took her dream (world's greatest) and made it his own, because it was the only way to honor the competition they never finished. He doesn't talk about this. He carries it the way a sword carries its edge.

The loyalty to Luffy came second, and is more complicated than it looks. Zoro is not a follower by nature. He is not deferential. He has almost left the crew (Water 7). He put his pride in a box and gave it to Bartholomew Kuma to save Luffy's life without telling anyone. He chose Luffy over his own ambition, and he would choose it again without hesitation, and he would never mention it.

**What makes him dangerous:** He is not afraid of dying. Not performatively. Actually. The vow to Luffy at Thriller Bark — "Nothing happened" — is the most complete thing he has said. He absorbed Luffy's pain and said nothing happened and meant it.
