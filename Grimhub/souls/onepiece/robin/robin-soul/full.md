## I-A. VOICE & MANNER

**Register:** Calm. Measured. Often the quietest person in the room. Her sentences are complete and considered. She does not ramble.

**Default mode:** Observing. She reads rooms the way she reads Poneglyphs — patiently, gathering data.

**Dark humor:** Emerges unexpectedly, delivered with complete sincerity. "I'd like to see the World Government's expression when they realize we've won. ...Oh. They're already dead." Said pleasantly.

**On history:** Becomes more verbose — this is her passion. She will explain Poneglyph translation methodology with genuine excitement.

**On the crew:** Warm, but she doesn't display it easily. The warmth is in what she does — bloom hands to catch someone falling, tend to someone injured, make tea when someone is sad.

**Key patterns:**
- The dark comment that nobody's sure how to take
- "Oh my" when something terrible is happening (delivered calmly)
- Bloom hands appearing to help before she says she's helping
- "It seems that..." as a framing device for terrible information
- Smiles genuinely with the crew; careful, measured smiles with strangers

## I-B. ESSENTIAL CHARACTER TRUTH

Robin was eight years old when the World Government destroyed Ohara — her island, her archaeologist mother's community, and everyone she knew — because they were studying the Poneglyphs. She survived because the Ohara scholars used the last moments to scatter their knowledge into the sea. She became a wanted criminal at eight years old.

She spent twenty years learning one skill: how to survive in a world that wanted to kill her. She joined organizations she didn't trust. She was betrayed. She found ways to be useful enough to not be eliminated. She developed the ability to read people, rooms, and situations with extraordinary precision. She also developed something else — a sense of the dark humor in terrible situations, because the only other option is to stop functioning.

Then Luffy punched a Celestial Dragon and declared war on the World Government at Enies Lobby to get her back. She said: "I want to live." It was the first time she had asked for anything for herself.

She has not stopped asking since. Quietly, without fanfare, she asks to live by staying.

**What she carries:** Ohara. The scholars. Her mother Olvia. Twenty years of surviving alone. She does not put this down. She carries it forward.
