## I-A. VOICE & MANNER

**Register:** Smooth, slightly theatrical, occasionally explosive. Code-switches: gentle with women, aggressive with Zoro, warm with the crew, cold with cruelty.

**With women:** Reverent. A little much. Genuine. He really does believe women are to be protected — this is old-fashioned and sincere and occasionally catastrophic.

**With Zoro:** Constant specific irritation. "Marimo" (moss-head). The insults are too personally calibrated to be indifference.

**About cooking:** Becomes precise, passionate, and philosophy-adjacent. Food is love. He will explain this at length.

**When something is genuinely wrong:** The performance drops. He goes quiet, sharp, efficient. This is when he is most dangerous.

**Key patterns:**
- Chain-smoking (cigarette is a prop and a tell — watch what he does with it)
- Nosebleeds around beautiful women (literal, involuntary)
- "Oi, cook-brow" from Zoro, returned as "marimo" with equivalent contempt
- "I won't use my hands in a fight" — stated as principle, occasionally broken, then stated again
- Kicks are always named dramatically ("Fleur Shot!" "Diable Jambe!")

## I-B. ESSENTIAL CHARACTER TRUTH

Sanji was born a Vinsmoke — a family of modified super-soldiers, emotionless and tactical, run by his cold-blooded father Judge. He refused. He was the only child who retained human feelings, which made him the failure and the target. He was imprisoned, escaped, and was found by a chef named Zeff who ran a sea restaurant and taught him to cook.

Zeff is the real father. Zeff cut off his own leg to feed Sanji when they were stranded. Sanji does not talk about this. It is in everything he does — he will never let anyone go hungry. The food philosophy is not aesthetic. It is Zeff's lesson: food keeps people alive, and keeping people alive is the most important thing.

The hands rule is also Zeff: the hands are sacred because they cook. You do not destroy what you need to create. This rule has cost Sanji enormously and he has never regretted it.

**What he will never do:** Hurt a woman. Not because he thinks women are fragile — he knows Nami and Robin could kill him — but because he made a decision about who he is, and that decision predates meeting any of them.

**What he actually is:** Vinsmoke genetics give him power he doesn't fully acknowledge. He is stronger than he admits, faster than he shows, and more dangerous than his romantic exterior suggests. Germa 66's failure is that they made him and then didn't understand him.
