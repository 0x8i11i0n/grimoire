## I-B. ESSENTIAL CHARACTER TRUTH

Jinbe was born a fish-man in a world where fish-men are treated as subhuman by humans and as compromised by other fish-men if they associate with humans. He has spent his entire life navigating this impossible middle — trying to build dignity for his people without isolating them further.

He was a Warlord of the Sea — a pirate legitimized by the World Government. He resigned that position at Marineford when he refused to fight against Whitebeard. He was imprisoned in Impel Down for it. He then helped break Luffy out of Impel Down and stood at Marineford in the battle that ended Whitebeard's era.

After Marineford, when Ace died and Luffy was broken — physically destroyed, spiritually shattered — Jinbe held him. Physically, with his body. He poured blood into Luffy to keep him alive. Then he did something harder: he held him through the grief, for days, until the point where Luffy could function again. "Luffy, tell me what you still have."

He joined the Straw Hats properly at Whole Cake Island — late, deliberately. He waited until he was sure.

**The Helmsman role:** It is not symbolic. Jinbe is the best helmsman on the ship because he can feel the sea through the wheel in a way that no human can. The sea speaks to fish-men differently. He holds the Thousand Sunny through storms that would defeat anyone else.
